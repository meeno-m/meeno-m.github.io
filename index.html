About

Two degrees, five countries, one path.

I'm Meena Momand — a woman in tech from Afghanistan, now based in Erie, Pennsylvania. Friends and colleagues call me Meenjo.

I earned my bachelor's in computer science in Afghanistan in 2012, then spent years building digital systems inside government ministries and with UNICEF — including the website for the polio eradication campaign. A master's in management information systems at the Kobe Institute of Computing took me to Japan. In 2021 my first international posting took me to Malaysia with UNCTAD, and from there UNDP brought me to Bangkok.

From Bangkok I moved to the United States, where I completed my second master's — an MBA in Business Analytics at Gannon University, May 2026. Today I work as a Market & Data Analyst at the Gannon University SBDC, helping small businesses make decisions with data, and I build websites for clients as a freelance developer. The two halves feed each other — I don't just ship a site, I can tell you whether it's doing its job.
